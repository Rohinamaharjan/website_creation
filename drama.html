Introduction

Play and drama and the use of puppets are effective in helping the young children to learn to be creative and express themselves emotionally. These activities help children in role play, in expressing their feelings and also help in the improvement of their imagination. However, performing arts, particularly drama and puppetry have the importance of improving on the child’s ability to communicate as well as social skills.

Resources, Materials, and Digital Technologies

  • Puppets:(Hand puppets, finger puppets, shadow puppets)
  • Costumes and props: Different thematic disguises for the characters and accessories that will contribute to the creation of interesting outfits.
  • Storybooks and scripts: Youth-appropriate story plots and basic plot lines and directions for the performances.
  • Audio-visual equipment: Recreation of performances to capture vital data in order to also recap on performances through record and playback devices.
  • Digital storytelling apps and software: Movable aid to support brands for the sake of orienting and dramatization.

Learning Experiences

0-2 Years

Simple Puppet Play

It is recommended that for the infants, use soft and colorful hand puppets that will make the children participate in simple stories and songs. Persuade them to extend their hands and feel the puppets. It also helps in growth of sensory –motor skills in young children as they learn in a fun way.

2-3 Years

Role Play with Costumes

It is important to have different costumes and props so that the toddler gets to switch from being one role to another or character to another. Tell them to portray basic scenes and events, either in their own lives or anyone else’s. During this stage, simulation of the roles help in the development of the aspects of socialeness and appreciation of roles.

3-5 Years

Puppet Shows

Let children to develop their own idea of the puppet shows and use various types of puppets and small sized scenario of a theatre. It helps in developing storytelling skills, group work efficiency and also public speaking skills to present to the audience in the best way possible.

6-8 Years

Script Writing and Performance

Guide children in writing their own short scripts and performing them using puppets or as live-action plays. This activity enhances literacy, creativity, and public speaking skills.

Teaching Strategies

All these activities help them in areas of collaboration and communication as they develop to higher forms of social interactions.Early childhood teachers can implement the following pedagogical practices to foster creativity in drama and puppetry:

  • Encourage open-ended play and exploration
  • Provide a variety of materials and resources
  • Facilitate group activities and collaborative projects
  • Offer guidance and support while allowing for autonomy
  • Use storytelling and role-playing to enhance learning
